Question

Answer

This is an excellent and important diagnostic question. The answer involves both a timing limitation and a prompt framework deficiency. Let me analyze precisely why.

Root Cause Analysis

The Data at 06:50 vs. 06:52

Adam posted at 06:52:43. Your script ran with -t 06:50. These 2–3 minutes are critical.

At 06:50, this rule fires correctly based on the data — delta IS negative AND price IS declining. The AI sees a textbook bearish-consistent pattern.

The 3 Things Adam Saw That the AI Framework Misses

1. Selling Deceleration (Rate of Change)

The micro delta sequence was: -6,519 → -1,955 → -2,148 → -4,098. While not monotonically decreasing, the average selling intensity dropped ~55% from the opening wave. Adam reads the tape in real-time and feels this deceleration — each sell wave is failing to generate proportional downside. The prompt framework has no rule for "aggregate delta is bearish, but selling velocity is decelerating across micro intervals."

2. Dense Passive Bid Absorption at Fixed Support

The absorption data showed 40+ absorption events each at 7526.50, 7527.00, and 7527.25, all classified as "看多吸收" (bullish absorption). Despite -14,720 net delta, these bids held. The price bounced off this zone repeatedly. The prompt has a "吸收信号的双面性" rule, but it doesn't override the dominant "空头打压" classification when delta AND price are both bearish.

3. PM Context as Prior Bias

PM had +12,108 delta with a clean +7.00 advance. The RTH opening selloff should be evaluated as a potential shakeout against this bullish prior, not as a new bearish trend. The prompt mentions PM data but doesn't have an explicit rule: "If PM was strongly bullish and RTH opens with selling that fails to break below PM support, treat this as a shakeout, not a new trend."
